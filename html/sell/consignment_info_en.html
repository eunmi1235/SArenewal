Art consignment

  • A global platform
    Established in 1998, Seoul Auction is Korea’s oldest and largest art auction house. It holds the largest share of the Korean art market and exhibition facilities in Seoul (Pyeongchang-dong head office, Gangnam Center) and Busan. It also opened a permanent facility, SA+, in Hong Kong to improve its accessibility as an art platform to collectors worldwide.

  • 20 years of expertise
    Since its founding, Seoul Auction has constantly proposed new standards for the value of art through its auctions. A team of specialists in diverse fields ensure, through a detailed appraisal process, that your artwork’s value is recognized in the art market and that your art will have the opportunity to be displayed in cities worldwide.

  • Record-breaking sales
    Through its many auctions, Seoul Auction has witnessed record-breaking hammer prices for Korean and foreign artists. Price records for Kim WhanKi, who currently holds the record for the highest hammer price for a Korean artist, as well as Lee JungSeop and Park SooKeun were all made possible by Seoul Auction. It is also an emerging powerhouse in ancient Korean art, as evidenced by the high prices secured for white celadon “moon jars,” folk paintings, and various paintings and calligraphic works.
Consignment process
STEP 01
Submission of application
A team of seasoned specialists in ancient, modern, and contemporary Korean art as well as foreign art will conduct a thorough appraisal—from the customer’s perspective—of an artwork submitted by a customer for consignment.
Seoul Auction is always ready to accept the artworks provided by customers not only for our major auctions, which are held once each quarter, but also online and special auctions.
In order for your artwork to be featured in an auction, you are required to apply for consideration for consignment.
Send email with basic information on artwork
art@seoulauction.com (Artwork Auction Team)
Title format
Artwork consignment_Your name (e.g. Artwork consignment_Hong Gildong)
Content of email (please follow this order)
  • 1. Your name
  • 2. Your contact information
  • 3. Artist’s name
  • 4. Title of artwork
  • 5. Dimensions, medium, year created
  • 6. Brief explanation of how you came to own the artwork
  • 7. Photos of artwork: full-view and close-up photos of important features (e.g. artist’s signature)
  • A reply will be sent to the email address from which the above information was sent. Please make sure to include your name (name of consignor) in the title.
Apply via website

After logging in, you can apply for artwork consignment through “1:1 Inquiry.”

Apply for art consignment

  • A Seoul Auction employee will contact you after checking that both the email and website application have been completed.
  • You can view the reply at “Member Information > 1:1 Inquiry.”
STEP 02
Initial screening
A document screening will be conducted by a specialist in the era and/or genre of the artwork. You will receive a reply via email or Seoul Auction’s website (1:1 Inquiry) within seven business days of submission.

STEP 03
Appraisal and contract signing
  • 01
    Discussion with Seoul Auction employee on artwork after completion of document screening
  • 02
    Receipt of physical artwork by Seoul Auction (shipped by consignor)
  • 03
    Appraisal of physical artwork
  • 04
    Signing of consignment contract after determination of artwork’s price (price at which it will be featured in an auction) through negotiation with consignor
STEP 04
Payment
Once an artwork is sold, the Seoul Auction employee in charge will mail a successful bid notification to both the consignor and the successful bidder. After the successful bidder makes the payment, the artwork is sent to him/her. The consignment commission is also paid at this time.
